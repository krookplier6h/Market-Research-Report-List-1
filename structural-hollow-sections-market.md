<p><p>The structural hollow sections market is divided into two primary types: hot formed and cold formed. Hot formed sections are created by heating metal above its recrystallization temperature, allowing for complex shapes and thicker walls, resulting in enhanced mechanical properties. Cold formed sections are made by shaping steel at room temperature, offering greater precision and smoother finishes. Both types are widely used in construction and infrastructure due to their strength, versatility, and efficiency in load-bearing applications.</p></p>
