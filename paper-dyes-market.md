<p><p>The paper dyes market comprises several types of dyes used in papermaking and coloring processes. Sulfur dyes are known for their lightfastness and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for dark shades. Direct dyes are water-soluble and offer convenience, providing vibrant colors with good affinity for cellulose fibers. Basic dyes are cationic and produce bright colors, ideal for paper with high dye uptake. Acid dyes are mainly used for dyeing protein-based fibers, contributing vivid shades in specialty paper applications.</p></p>
